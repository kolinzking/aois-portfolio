# Phase 10 Looking Forward

Authoring status: authored

Phase 10 is authored.

What AOIS has gained so far:

- modality catalog
- media type allowlist
- file validation and size gates
- consent and privacy gates
- PII redaction for media
- accessibility alternatives
- transcripts and extracted text
- cross-modal consistency checks
- multimodal evidence and confidence handling
- text fallback
- platform and release integration for multimodal paths
- central, edge-online, and offline-edge placement
- device profiles and resource budgets
- offline cache, sync, and freshness controls
- observability buffers
- update channel and rollback controls
- access and release gates for edge deployments
- authorized red-team scope
- sanitized adversarial scenario catalog
- prompt, retrieval, tool, output, cache, fallback, and policy-confusion tests
- telemetry, evidence, severity, mitigation, and regression gates
- governance-verified computer-use planning
- local synthetic computer-use environments
- approval, manual handoff, preview, budget, stop control, rollback, audit, redaction, operator watch, red-team clearance, release, and access gates

Next corpus work:

- end-to-end corpus review
- source currency review
- full validator and simulator pass
- safety-boundary review
- teaching-sequence review

The current bridge is from Phase 10 authoring into corpus review. AOIS-P can now
model whether non-text signals may be analyzed, where constrained inference may
run, how adversarial tests should be scoped, and whether computer-use action
plans are governed before execution. The next job is proving the whole corpus is
coherent, safe, runnable, and ready for live teaching review.
