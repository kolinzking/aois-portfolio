# AOIS Study Pacing

This file answers one practical question:

How long will the curriculum take if you study for a fixed number of minutes each day?

These are honest estimates for focused work, not passive reading time.

## Assumptions

- you are actively building, not only reading
- you run the commands
- you do the labs
- you do not skip the mastery checkpoints
- you keep the repo and notes in sync

## Total Program Estimate

Current full-corpus target:

- approximately `180-280 focused hours` end to end

This is intentionally broad because later phases vary in difficulty and infrastructure overhead.

## Time By Daily Pace

| Daily pace | Weekly pace | 180 hours | 220 hours | 280 hours |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 30 min/day | 3.5 hrs/week | ~52 weeks | ~63 weeks | ~80 weeks |
| 45 min/day | 5.25 hrs/week | ~34 weeks | ~42 weeks | ~54 weeks |
| 60 min/day | 7 hrs/week | ~26 weeks | ~32 weeks | ~40 weeks |
| 90 min/day | 10.5 hrs/week | ~18 weeks | ~21 weeks | ~27 weeks |
| 120 min/day | 14 hrs/week | ~13 weeks | ~16 weeks | ~20 weeks |
| 180 min/day | 21 hrs/week | ~9 weeks | ~11 weeks | ~14 weeks |

## Recommended Interpretation

If you want deep retention without burnout:

- `45-90 minutes/day` is the most sustainable range

If you want aggressive progress:

- `120-180 minutes/day` is strong, but only if you keep quality high

## Phase 0 Estimate

Phase 0 is foundational and should not be rushed.

Estimated Phase 0 time:

- `20-35 focused hours`

At different daily paces:

| Daily pace | Time for Phase 0 |
|---|---:|
| 30 min/day | ~6-10 weeks |
| 45 min/day | ~4-7 weeks |
| 60 min/day | ~3-5 weeks |
| 90 min/day | ~2-4 weeks |
| 120 min/day | ~2-3 weeks |

## Rule

Do not optimize for calendar speed alone.
Optimize for durable understanding.

The correct unit of progress is not:

- days spent

It is:

- versions genuinely mastered
