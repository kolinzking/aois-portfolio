# AOIS Corpus Authoring Plan

This document exists to keep corpus authoring disciplined.

The goal is not to leave behind 34 placeholders.
The goal is to produce a full mastery-grade lesson corpus that can be reviewed end to end before teaching begins.

## Authoring Order

1. Phase 0
2. Phase 1
3. Phase 2
4. Phase 3
5. Phase 4
6. Phase 5
7. Phase 6
8. Phase 7
9. Phase 8
10. Phase 9
11. Phase 10

## Why This Order

The teaching quality of later phases depends on the conceptual precision of earlier phases.

The higher the system climbs:

- the more the learner depends on old mental models
- the more expensive shallow earlier notes become

So the early phases must be right before the late phases are trusted.

## Review Gates

Before a phase is considered complete for corpus purposes:

1. Every version in the phase has a full lesson pack
2. Every version satisfies `VERSION-STANDARD.md`
3. The phase introduction and looking-forward files are fully authored
4. The phase can be explained as one connected capability jump
5. No backbone domain is brushed past in that phase

## Audit Standard

At the end of each phase authoring pass, review for:

- shallow sections
- missing expected outputs
- missing failure exposure
- missing architecture defense
- missing four-layer drills
- missing four-level system explanation drills
- broken continuity into the next phase
